How does the Army calculate body fat?

The "Tape Test" Method

The Army Body Composition Program (ABCP) uses a circumference-based method known as the "tape test." How does the army calculate body fat exactly? It compares your height against circumference measurements of your body.

Standard Methods:

  • New One-Site Test (2023): Only measures the waist (at the navel) relative to body weight. This is often more favorable for muscular soldiers.
  • Old Multi-Site Test:
    • Males: Neck and Waist (Abdomen) measurements.
    • Females: Neck, Waist, and Hip measurements.

This army fat body calculator supports both methods so you can see which one gives you the best result.

How to Calculate Army Body Fat (Measuring Guide)

To get accurate results, follow these measuring tips:

  • Neck: Measure just below the larynx (Adam's apple). Keep the tape flat.
  • Waist (Male): Measure across the navel (belly button).
  • Waist (Female): Measure at the narrowest point of the abdomen.
  • Hips (Female): Measure at the widest protrusion of the buttocks.

Note: Round all measurements to the nearest half-inch (0.5) or centimeter for the most accurate army apft body fat calculator result.

How accurate is the Army Body Fat Calculator?

A common question soldiers ask is: how accurate is the army body fat calculator?

Scientific Accuracy

The tape test is an estimation tool. It has a margin of error of roughly 3-5% compared to DEXA scans or hydrostatic weighing. However, it is the only official method used for administrative actions in the Army.

New vs. Old Test

The New One-Site Test was introduced to be fairer to muscular soldiers who were previously penalized by the neck/waist ratio. If you fail the one-site test, you are usually authorized to take the multi-site test for confirmation.

The Army does not use army body fat as the final standard. They use body fat percentage. However, if your weight is low enough according to screening tables, you do not need to be taped.
