What are BMR, TDEE, and Macros?

BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the calories your body burns at rest. TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) adds your daily activity to estimate the calories you need to maintain weight. Your macronutrients (protein, carbs, fat) are split from those calories to match goals like fat loss, maintenance, or muscle gain.

How we calculate your numbers

  • BMR: Mifflin–St Jeor equation (more accurate for most adults).
  • TDEE: BMR × activity level (sedentary to super active).
  • Macros: Choose Balanced (30/40/30), Low Carb (40/25/35), or High Protein (40/30/30).

Tips for better results

  • Pick the activity level that matches most days of your week.
  • Recalculate after weight changes of 5–10 lb (2–5 kg) or routine changes.
  • Use a modest calorie deficit (250–500 kcal/day) for sustainable fat loss.

How to calculate your BMI

  1. Choose units: Metric (kg/cm) or Imperial (lb/ft/in).
  2. Measure your height and weight accurately (ideally the same day/time).
  3. Apply the formula for your unit system:
    • Metric: BMI = weight (kg) / [height (m)]²
    • Imperial: BMI = 703 × weight (lb) / [height (in)]²
  4. Interpret the result (adults 20+):
    • Underweight: < 18.5
    • Healthy weight: 18.5–24.9
    • Overweight: 25–29.9
    • Obesity: ≥ 30

Tip: For a healthy weight range, reverse the formula using your height: min = 18.5 × height(m)², max = 24.9 × height(m)². Note: BMI is a screening tool and may not reflect body composition for athletes, pregnant people, or all body types.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which BMR formula do you use?

We use the Mifflin–St Jeor equation, widely regarded as most accurate for estimating resting energy needs in adults.

What is TDEE?

TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is your daily calorie burn including activity. It’s calculated as BMR × an activity multiplier.

Which units can I use?

Both metric (kg/cm) and imperial (lb/ft/in). Switch units any time and the calculator adjusts fields accordingly.

Are my results private and secure?

Yes. All calculations run locally in your browser. Your data never leaves your device.

What macro split should I choose?

Balanced (30% protein, 40% carbs, 30% fat) suits most people. Low Carb (40/25/35) may help appetite control. High Protein (40/30/30) supports muscle retention. Pick what you can stick to.

How often should I recalculate?

Recalculate after weight changes of ~2–5 kg (5–10 lb), when your activity level changes, or every 4–6 weeks during a program.

What is a healthy BMI?

For most adults, 18.5–24.9. BMI is a screening tool and doesn’t capture body composition; consider waist and strength metrics too.

Is this medical advice?

No. These tools are for education and planning. For diagnosis or treatment, consult a qualified healthcare professional.
