How to create a Bitcoin QR code

  1. Enter your Bitcoin address (bc1, 1, or 3).
  2. Optional: add Amount (BTC) and a Message (e.g., “Donation” or invoice note).
  3. Customize colors and optionally upload a logo.
  4. Click “Generate” to preview, then download PNG or PDF.
  5. Test by scanning with a wallet app (e.g., BlueWallet, Muun, Exodus, Trust Wallet).

Tip: Keep strong contrast (dark foreground on light background) for best scan results.

Best practices

  • Use high contrast (black foreground, white background) and avoid busy backgrounds.
  • If you use a logo, keep it modest (10–25% of QR width) to preserve scanability.
  • Validate your address and double-check the encoded amount before sharing.
  • Test across wallets and devices. Keep a plain version (no logo) as a fallback.
  • Fees are chosen by the sender’s wallet and are not embedded in the QR.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it private and secure?

Yes. Everything runs locally in your browser. We don’t upload or store your data.

What is BIP21?

BIP21 is the standard Bitcoin URI format that encodes address plus optional amount and message for wallets to read.

Which addresses are supported?

bech32 (bc1), P2PKH (1), and P2SH (3) formats are supported by most wallets.

Can I add amount and message?

Yes, enter an amount in BTC and an optional message (e.g., invoice or donation note).

Lightning supported?

This tool is for on chain Bitcoin QRs (BIP21). Lightning invoices (lnurl/bolt11) are not included.

My QR doesn’t scan help?

Use high contrast, reduce logo size, increase QR size, and test with multiple wallet apps.
